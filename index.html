Breast Cancer

According to the World Health Organization, breast cancer is the most common cancer globally, affecting over 2.3 million women in 2020 alone.

Regular mammography screening and early detection can reduce mortality by up to 30%.

Lung Cancer

Lung cancer is responsible for more deaths worldwide than any other type of cancer, with over 1.8 million deaths reported in 2020 (WHO).

About 85% of lung cancer cases are linked to tobacco use, making prevention efforts like smoking cessation critical.

Colon Cancer

Colon (colorectal) cancer is the third most common cancer worldwide. In 2020, it caused nearly 1 million deaths (Global Cancer Observatory).

Screening such as colonoscopy can detect polyps early and reduce the risk of cancer development by up to 70%.
